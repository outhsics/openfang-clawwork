# Rotating Cleanup Schedule Memo

## To
Administrative Services Staff and Volunteer Support Team

## From
Operations Coordination Office

## Date
2026-03-06

## Subject
Implementation of Rotating Cleanup Schedule and Disruption Procedures

### Purpose

Starting next week, the branch will use a rotating cleanup schedule to keep shared spaces usable, reduce confusion over ownership, and make it easier to recover quickly after busy service periods, events, and volunteer activity.

### Why we are changing the process

The previous ad hoc process created three recurring problems:

- cleanup tasks were handled inconsistently between teams and shifts
- volunteers did not always know which tasks were still open
- disruptions such as meetings, training sessions, or large visitor volume caused missed cleanup work with no clear backup owner

The rotating schedule fixes this by assigning a clear weekly lead, a backup contact, and a simple escalation path.

### What staff should expect

- each week has one primary cleanup lead and one backup lead
- the assigned lead confirms that the checklist has been completed by end of day Friday
- the backup lead steps in when the primary lead is out, reassigned, or unavailable
- volunteers may assist with low-risk tasks, but final sign-off remains with staff

### Disruption procedures

If a planned task cannot be completed on the scheduled day, staff should use the following order:

1. Notify the weekly lead as soon as the disruption is known.
2. Move the task to the backup lead or the next available day within the same week.
3. If the task affects health, safety, visitor readiness, or shared equipment, escalate to the Operations Coordinator immediately.
4. Record the change in the editable schedule so the next team can see the updated status.

### Escalation rules

Escalate immediately when any of the following apply:

- supply shortage prevents completion
- a shared public area is left in unusable condition
- a task has rolled over twice without completion
- a volunteer needs instruction beyond the posted checklist

### Recordkeeping

The attached editable schedule should be used as the source of truth for:

- weekly lead assignment
- backup assignment
- completion status
- disruption notes
- follow-up actions

### Effective date

The rotating schedule takes effect on Monday, 2026-03-09. Team leads should review the first four weeks of assignments by end of day Friday.
